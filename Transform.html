· TRANSFORM ·

The urban transformation space, that is the Superkilen, wedges through one of the most diverse and socially

challenged neighborhood in Coppenhagen (Nørrebro). It is a mile-long park that commemorates the diversity of

the community through a joint-effort with the 60 different nationalities of the people living in the area – to

be engaged in a sort of collection of global objects found in their places of origin. The park showcase exercise

gear from LA to Islamic tiled fountains in Morocco, curvy benches from Brazil to neon signs from Qatar and Russia.

Each object is accompanied by a stainless steel plaque the describes what the object is and where it is from, and

written in Danish and in the language(s) of the origin.

Rather than contributing to the stark image of a seemingly homogenous Denmark, the Superkilen park was considered

a surrealist collection of urban diversity that inevitably amplifies the true nature of the local neighborhood.

· ARGUMENTS ·

A major concern facing the park design team, after project completion, was the distaste towards the use of
synthetic artifacts and features - contributing to Superkilen's beautification. Some of the long-serving residents of
the surrounding neighbourhoods complained about the excessive use of fake grass and somewhat the slightly skewed Western
depictions of eastern societies, through art. Though the latter was not in fact a cause for concern, it did raise some eyebrows.

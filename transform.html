From the beginning, a convent has dismantled on the ground where the parasol locates, a bounded market

consisting of several buildings functioned here during the 19th Century until 1973 when the ensemble was

knock down. From that point on, the square was becoming a car park and possibility to construct an underground

carpark were suggested. In the progress of excavating the centre for constructing the underground car park,

a range of heritages had found which came from Imperial Rome and Late Antique and Islamic city. At the moment,

the square and car park were looks abandoned and old that the renovation and transformation were seems needed.

Then after Metropol Parasol replaced the archaeological site completely, it did attract residents and more

visitors for the waffle-like appearance and functions of the building that the huge parasol provides an archaeology

museum which exhibits the heritages being discovered underground, and a farmer market, plaza, restaurants,

bars and a panorama terrace on the upper level of the parasols.
